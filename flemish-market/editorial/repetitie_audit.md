# Repetition Audit

This is an initial adaptation audit, not a completed line edit.

## Motifs To Preserve But Compress

- AI is a blessing when it serves, dangerous when it becomes master.
- Output is not the same as understanding.
- The map matters more than the generated answer.
- Cognitive debt forms when production outruns comprehension.
- Conscious delegation preserves responsibility and judgment.
- Skill should migrate upward when tasks move to machines.
- The classroom should demand insight, not merely ban AI.
- Work carries identity, structure, dignity, and social function beyond income.
- Governance must address ownership, access, incentives, and collective risk.
- Worthy ancestors must prepare a successor without becoming passive.

## Adaptation Rule

Keep the strongest occurrence of each motif in each part of the book. Cut weaker echoes unless they perform a new structural function.
