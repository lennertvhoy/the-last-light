## 3. Topic Summary

This is a comprehensive training deck aimed at ICT helpdesk workers and broader IT professionals who want to use generative AI productively and responsibly. It spans AI fundamentals (history, rule-based vs. machine learning, generative AI, neural networks, tokens), the impact of AI on work (productivity gains, the "nivelleringseffect" or leveling effect, cognitive atrophy, organizational impact), practical prompting and evaluation skills (the 4C rule, improving weak prompts, checking for hallucinations and bias), an overview of current AI tools, reusable workflows, an introduction to vibe coding, and governance/security/policy considerations. The structure is modular, with audience-specific packages and workshop blocks suggested.

## 4. Target Audience

- **Primary:** ICT helpdesk employees and IT support staff.
- **Secondary:** trainers and teachers, knowledge workers, managers, beginners, and students.

## 5. Key Takeaways

- Generative AI has a long pre-history but became widely accessible from 2022 onward; it predicts likely next tokens rather than "understanding" the world.
- AI tends to produce a **leveling effect**: large productivity gains for less experienced workers, smaller gains for experts, which changes competence dynamics in teams.
- Major risks include **cognitive atrophy**, over-reliance, **hallucinations**, security vulnerabilities, **bias**, lack of transparency, and compliance issues.
- Strong prompting follows the **4C rule**: Context, Constraints, Criteria, and Checkvraag (check/reflection question).
- AI output should be evaluated across correctness, efficiency/best practices, security, bias, transparency, and compliance/ethics.
- **Human-in-the-loop** and **human-on-the-loop** models are essential; AI is an assistant, not a decision-maker.
- Practical IT workflows covered include research, email/writing, troubleshooting, documentation, meeting/webinar summarization, script development, and presentation creation.
- **Vibe coding** tools lower the barrier for turning ideas into scripts, GUIs, apps, and documentation, but still require review and testing.
- Organization-wide rollout requires clear frameworks, training, adapted procedures, and ongoing quality control.
- Prompts should be documented and periodically reviewed for continuous improvement.

## 6. The Last Light Themes

- **Human agency:** Repeated emphasis on keeping humans in or on the loop, and on "human expertise heruitvinden" (reinventing human expertise).
- **Conscious delegation:** Clear framing of when to use AI, when not to, and what controls are mandatory.
- **AI collaboration:** "Mens + A.I. werken best als tandem" (human + AI work best as a tandem).
- **Cognitive atrophy:** Directly addressed as a core risk when thinking, problem-solving, and memory are outsourced to AI.

- **State Driven Development (StateDD):** Not explicitly named, but strongly aligned through its emphasis on structured frameworks, documented procedures, compliance (EU AI Act), validation loops, and traceable governance.
- **Governance and safety:** Includes dedicated sections on security, privacy, copyright, EU AI Act, bias, transparency, and organizational rollout.
