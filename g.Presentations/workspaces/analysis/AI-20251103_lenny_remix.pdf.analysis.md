## 3. Topic Summary

This is a comprehensive training deck that introduces ICT helpdesk professionals to generative AI. It covers AI history and basic concepts (ANI/AGI/ASI, tokens, probability, limitations), the impact of AI on the workplace (productivity gains, the "nivelleringseffect," cognitive atrophy), practical prompt engineering using the 4C method, evaluation criteria for AI output, documentation and continuous improvement, reusable workflows, vibe coding/tooling, and governance topics including security, privacy, bias, human-in-the-loop, and the EU AI Act.

## 4. Target Audience

- Primary: **ICT helpdesk staff and system administrators** who want to use generative AI safely and effectively.
- Secondary: trainers/docenten, knowledge workers, managers, and beginners/students, as reflected in the audience-specific packages (08.01–08.05).

## 5. Key Takeaways

- AI has evolved from rule-based systems (1960s–80s) through machine learning to today’s generative AI (from 2022), which predicts likely next tokens rather than understanding truth.
- AI delivers the biggest productivity gains for less experienced workers (the "nivelleringseffect"), while high-skilled workers see little to no gain.
- AI has a "hobbelige grens" (jagged boundary): it can be strong in complex tasks and weak in simple ones, so human + AI tandem works best.
- Intensive AI use risks **cognitive atrophy** — reduced critical thinking, problem solving, and memory — if professionals delegate too much of their thinking.
- Effective prompting requires preparation: define context, data/security level, prompt goal, and role before writing the prompt.
- The **4C-regel** structures prompts: **Context**, **Constraints**, **Criteria**, and **Checkvraag**.
- AI output must be evaluated for correctness/hallucinations, efficiency/best practices, security, bias, transparency, and compliance (GDPR, ISO27001, NIS2).
- Documentation and version control of prompts/outputs are essential for reproducibility, auditing, and team learning.
- Reusable workflows include research, email/writing, troubleshooting, documentation, meeting-to-output, scripting, presentations, and vibe coding.
- Governance requires clear policies, human-in-the-loop / human-on-the-loop controls, awareness of the EU AI Act, and continuous review.

## 6. The Last Light Themes

- **Human agency and staying human:** Strong emphasis on cognitive atrophy and the risk that AI replaces parts of thinking and writing, not just supports them.
- **Conscious delegation:** Repeated message that AI is an assistant, not a decision maker; humans must verify, especially for configurations and changes.
- **Human + AI collaboration:** The "tandem" model and the jagged-boundary concept frame AI as a collaborator with known limits.
- **State Driven Development (StateDD):** Governance section, documentation/versioning of prompts, labeling risk levels, runbooks, compliance with EU AI Act, and structured evaluation criteria mirror StateDD’s emphasis on explicit state, controls, and auditability.
- **Staying relevant when AI can do more:** The nivelleringseffect and the call to "reinvent human expertise" directly address how workers remain valuable.
- **Trust, transparency, and ethics:** Sections on bias, transparency, compliance, and security vulnerabilities tie into the book’s themes of responsible AI use.
