# Glossary of Key Terms\n\n*   **Centaur**: A strategic approach in human-AI collaboration where the human expert acts as a manager, delegating "inside the frontier" tasks to AI while preserving their own role for more nuanced problems; maintaining their role as the "rider."\n\n*   **Chinese Room**: A system or entity that processes inputs and produces outputs by following rules, without genuine understanding or comprehension of the meaning. It appears to understand from the outside, but lacks true internal awareness.\n\n*   **Cognitive Atrophy**: The erosion or decay of cognitive abilities due to over-reliance on external tools and technology, leading to deskilling and reduced mental capacity for tasks once performed internally.\n\n*   **Cyborg**: A strategic approach in human-AI collaboration characterized by radical integration, where the human workflow is deeply intertwined with AI, forming a single, hybrid entity, often adopted by novices for significant productivity boosts.\n\n*   **Semantic Apocalypse**: The point at which meaning itself breaks down, where the distinction between understanding and its simulation becomes meaningless, and comprehension becomes obsolete.\n\n*   **The Leveling Effect**: A phenomenon where access to powerful AI assistance significantly reduces the performance gap between experts and novices, raising the floor of performance but making expert advantage less relevant.
