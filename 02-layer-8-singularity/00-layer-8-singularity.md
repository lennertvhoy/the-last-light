Clarke "Any sufficiently advanced incompetence is indistinguishable from malice."   — Grey's Law "Any sufficiently advanced AI is indistinguishable from unemployment."   — Silicon Valley Proverb, circa 2025 The Stack Inverts For decades, computer scientists have used the OSI model to understand networked communication. Seven layers of abstraction, from the physical transmission of bits to the application layer where humans interact. It's a beautiful hierarchy, each layer hiding complexity from the ones above, each dependent on the ones below. And sitting on top, unofficial but universally acknowledged, was Layer 8: the human user. The chaos agent. The source of all problems. PEBKAC—Problem Exists Between Keyboard And Chair. ID-10-T error. The weak link in an otherwise perfect system. We were the bug. The inefficiency. The thing that broke the beautiful machinery with our fat fingers and faulty logic. Were. Past tense. Because something unprecedented has happened. The stack has inverted. Humans aren't the problem anymore—we're being solved. We're not Layer 8, the chaotic users breaking the system. We're becoming Layer 9, the obsolete directors of systems that no longer need direction. And AI? AI is the new Layer 8. The universal translator. The omnipotent intermediary. The layer that finally makes the whole stack work by removing the need for humans to understand it.
