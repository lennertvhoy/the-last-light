# Appendix L: AI Skepticism

This appendix provides an overview of the primary "Tool-AI" proponents, who are skeptical of the hype surrounding AGI and existential risk.

## Yann LeCun: The Engineer's Pragmatism

LeCun argues that current AI systems are severely limited, lacking common sense, reasoning, and planning abilities. He believes the focus on AGI is a distraction from building safe and reliable AI systems.

## Andrew Ng: The Economic Pragmatist

Ng compares AI to electricity, a general-purpose technology that is transformative but ultimately a neutral tool. He argues that the hype around AGI is often used for fundraising and distracts from real-world applications.

## Melanie Mitchell: The Cognitive Scientist's Warning

Mitchell argues that the primary danger of AI is not that it will become superintelligent, but that we will overestimate its capabilities and give it too much autonomy. She emphasizes the "brittleness" of current AI systems.

## Rodney Brooks: The Embodiment Hypothesis

Brooks argues that true intelligence requires embodiment, the ability to interact with and learn from the physical environment. He rejects the idea of a disembodied "singularity."
