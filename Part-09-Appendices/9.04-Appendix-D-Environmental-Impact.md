# Appendix D: AI's Environmental Footprint

The environmental impact of AI is a growing concern. This appendix details the technical metrics used to assess this impact.

### Energy Consumption Metrics

*   **FLOPS (floating-point operations per second):** A fundamental metric for quantifying the computational workload of training and inference.
*   **Training Compute:** The energy consumed during training scales superlinearly with model size. Training a large model can be equivalent to tens or hundreds of thousands of pounds of CO2 emissions.
*   **Inference Costs:** While training is a one-time event, inference occurs continuously at scale, and the cumulative energy consumption is substantial.
*   **Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE):** A metric for data center energy efficiency. PUEs typically range from 1.2 to 2.0, indicating significant energy loss in cooling and other infrastructure.

## Carbon Footprint Analysis

*   **Grid Composition:** The carbon footprint of AI depends on the energy mix of the data center's location.
*   **Water Consumption:** Large data centers require significant water for cooling. Some reports estimate thousands of liters of water are consumed for training a single large LLM.
*   **Hardware Lifecycle:** The manufacturing, transportation, and disposal of AI hardware also contribute significantly to the carbon footprint.
*   **Comparisons:** To contextualize AI's impact, the training of some large models has been equated to the lifetime emissions of several cars.
