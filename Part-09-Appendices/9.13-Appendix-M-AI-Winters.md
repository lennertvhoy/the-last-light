# Appendix M: A Brief History of AI Winters

The history of AI is marked by periods of intense hype and optimism, followed by disillusionment and funding cuts, known as "AI winters."

## The First AI Winter (1974-1980)

Triggered by the failure of early AI systems to live up to their hype, the limitations of available hardware, and the Lighthill Report, which was highly critical of the progress in AI.

## The Second AI Winter (1987-1993)

Triggered by the collapse of the market for specialized AI hardware and software (Lisp machines), which were expensive and difficult to program.

## The Current Wave

The current wave of AI development is driven by large datasets, powerful new algorithms, and cheap, powerful hardware. Whether the promises of AGI will be fulfilled remains to be seen, but the history of AI winters should serve as a cautionary tale.
