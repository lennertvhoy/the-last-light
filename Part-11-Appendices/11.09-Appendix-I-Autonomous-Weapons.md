# Appendix I: Autonomous Weapons

Autonomous Weapons Systems (AWS) are military systems that can independently select and engage targets without human intervention. This appendix provides a technical breakdown of current and future AWS.

### Current Systems

*   **Loitering Munitions:** "Suicide drones" that can loiter in an area, detect targets, and then autonomously attack.
*   **Defensive Systems:** Systems like CIWS on naval ships or Israel's Iron Dome operate with high levels of autonomy to defend against incoming threats.
*   **Swarm Coordination:** Multiple unmanned systems collaborating to achieve a mission through decentralized control and consensus algorithms.
*   **Target Identification:** The core AI component of AWS, using computer vision and machine learning to detect, classify, and track targets.

### Control Architectures

*   **Human-in-the-loop (HITL):** A human must make the final decision to engage a target.
*   **Human-on-the-loop (HOTL):** The system operates autonomously, but a human operator can intervene.
*   **Human-out-of-the-loop (HOOTL):** The system is fully autonomous, making all decisions without human intervention.

## Ethical Implications

The development of AWS raises profound ethical and legal questions regarding accountability, discrimination, and the potential for uncontrolled escalation. The technical capabilities of AWS are advancing rapidly, leading to increasing calls for international regulation to ensure meaningful human control over lethal force.
